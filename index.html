A Life Dedicated to Science & Service

From humble beginnings in Rameswaram to becoming one of India's most respected leaders

Early Life & Education

Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam came from a humble family. His father was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque, while his mother was a housewife. Despite financial constraints, young Kalam was determined to learn.

He graduated in physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, in 1954 and then studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology, graduating in 1960.

Scientific Career

Kalam joined DRDO in 1958 and later moved to ISRO in 1969, where he served as the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III). His work on developing ballistic missile technology earned him the title "Missile Man of India."

He played a pivotal role in India's nuclear tests at Pokhran in 1998, contributing significantly to India's defense capabilities and technological advancement.

Lasting Legacy

His vision continues to inspire India's youth and scientific community

Educational Impact

Dr. Kalam's passion for teaching and mentoring young minds led to the establishment of numerous educational initiatives. His interactions with students across the country sparked interest in science and technology among millions.

Scientific Advancement

His contributions to India's missile and space programs laid the foundation for the country's emergence as a major space power, with current achievements like Mars Orbiter Mission building upon his pioneering work.

Vision 2020

His book "India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium" outlined a roadmap for transforming India into a developed nation, inspiring policy makers and citizens alike.

Continuing Inspiration

Today, schools, institutions, and programs across India bear his name, ensuring that his values of scientific temperament, innovation, and service to humanity continue to inspire future generations.
